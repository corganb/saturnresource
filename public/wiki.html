Physical parameters, structure, active missions, and scientific data sources

Overview

Saturn is the sixth planet and second-largest in the Solar System. Famous for its extensive ring system composed mostly of water ice. It has 146 known moons including Titan (larger than Mercury with a thick atmosphere) and Enceladus (ejecting water geysers from a subsurface ocean).

Active missions

Pioneer 11 (NASA)
Flyby 1979
First spacecraft to visit Saturn.
Voyager 1 / 2 (NASA)
Flybys 1980/1981
Detailed imagery of rings, atmosphere, and moons.
Cassini-Huygens (NASA/ESA)
Orbit 2004-2017
Most comprehensive study of Saturn system. Huygens probe landed on Titan in 2005 - first landing in the outer Solar System. Mission ended with Grand Finale dive into Saturn.
Dragonfly (NASA)
Planned launch 2028, arrival 2034
Rotorcraft mission to explore Titan's surface and atmosphere.

Notable features

Ring system
A, B, C, D, E, F, G rings plus numerous gaps (Cassini Division, Encke Gap). Mostly water ice with trace silicates.
Titan
Second largest moon in the Solar System. Only moon with a thick atmosphere. Surface has methane/ethane lakes and a possible subsurface water ocean.
Enceladus
Small moon with an icy shell covering a global subsurface ocean. Geysers at the south pole eject water containing organic compounds.
Hexagon storm
Six-sided jet stream at the north pole, ~30,000 km across.
Mimas
The "Death Star" moon, dominated by the Herschel crater.
